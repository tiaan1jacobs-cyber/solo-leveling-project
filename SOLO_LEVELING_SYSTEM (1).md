### **WEEKLY CHALLENGE (50 XP):**

**Challenge: "The Tail Detection Walk"**
- Time Required: 45 minutes
- Difficulty: Beginner
- Objective: Learn if you're being followed

**Instructions:**
1. **Preparation:**
   - Choose a route with: shops, restaurants, public spaces
   - Should take 30-40 minutes to walk normally
   - Note start time

2. **The Walk - Execute these surveillance detection moves:**

   **Move 1: The Pause (5 min mark)**
   - Stop suddenly to "tie your shoe" or "check phone"
   - Look behind you naturally while doing so
   - Who was close behind you? Note them.

   **Move 2: The Window Shop (10 min mark)**
   - Stop at store window
   - Use reflection to observe people behind you
   - Who stops? Who keeps walking? Who turns away?
   - Note suspicious behavior

   **Move 3: The Direction Change (15 min mark)**
   - Turn around and walk back the way you came
   - This is unnatural - normal people don't do this
   - Who also turns around?
   - Who suddenly finds something interesting to look at?

   **Move 4: The Store Entry (20 min mark)**
   - Enter a store or cafe
   - Wait inside for 3-5 minutes
   - Watch the entrance
   - Who comes in right after you?
   - Who waits outside?

   **Move 5: The Block Loop (25 min mark)**
   - Walk around one city block in a square
   - Complete the full loop back to where you started
   - Normal people don't do this
   - Who's still behind you after the loop?

   **Move 6: The Three-Right-Turns (30 min mark)**
   - Turn right three times (makes a loop)
   - If someone's still behind you after this, they're following
   - No one has a legitimate reason to make 3 right turns

3. **Documentation:**
   - Write a report:
     * Was anyone following you? (Probably not, this is training)
     * What did you learn about normal vs. suspicious behavior?
     * Which move was most effective?
     * How did it feel to be aware?

**Success Requirements:**
- Complete all 6 surveillance detection moves
- Take notes on observations
- Write after-action report
- XP Reward: 50 XP

- Time: 4 weeks
- Difficulty: Expert
- Objective: Learn to spot multiple-person surveillance

**Background:**
Real surveillance uses teams of 3-5 people who:
- Switch positions (never same person behind you)
- Use parallel streets (not behind you, beside you)
- Leapfrog (person ahead, person behind, they swap)
- Use vehicles and foot

**Week 1: Study**
- Research surveillance team tactics
- Watch videos on counter-surveillance
- Learn the "box" formation
- Understand leapfrogging
- Study communication methods

**Week 2: Practice Detection - Foot Surveillance**
During your daily movements:
1. **The Box Detection:**
   - Look for: Person ahead, person behind, one on each side
   - They maintain distance but stay with you
   - Make sudden moves: Do 4 people respond?

2. **The Leapfrog Spot:**
   - Person behind passes you, now ahead
   - Then falls back again
   - Or new person takes their place

3. **The Communication Tell:**
   - Touching ear (earpiece)
   - Talking to themselves (mic)
   - Hand signals
   - Unnatural phone use

**Week 3: Practice Detection - Vehicle Surveillance**
While driving/being driven:
1. **The Three-Car Pattern:**
   - One car behind
   - One parallel on next street
   - One ahead
   - They rotate positions

2. **The Turn Test:**
   - Make 4 right turns (full circle)
   - Same cars still there? Surveillance
   - Normal traffic wouldn't do this

3. **The Speed Trap:**
   - Sudden speed change
   - Do specific cars match your speed?

**Week 4: Advanced Counter-Surveillance Walk**
Execute complex route:
1. Use public transit (they have to follow)
2. Last-second entrances/exits (train door closing)
3. Busy areas (lose them in crowds)
4. Elevators (see who rushes in after you)
5. Reflective surfaces everywhere
6. Document: Anyone following?

**Deliverable:**
- Daily surveillance detection logs
- Description of any suspicious persons/vehicles
- Analysis of your counter-moves
- Assessment: Were you ever under surveillance? (Unlikely, but good training)

**Success Requirements:**
